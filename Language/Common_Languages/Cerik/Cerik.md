# Cerik

## The Practicality of an empirical language

Cerik originates from the area surrounding the capital city and its neighboring provinces. This region is known as Old Cerik and is the historic border of the origional country that has been slowly expanding its influence over many generations. Practically speaking this means that while most citizens of the empire primarily speak Cerik as their first language, the origional dialect of Cerik stays mainly in the central region. The citizens would actually speak a regional dialect of cerik based on the historically local languages. For example, Ardulian is the origional language of the Ardulian Cressant, It's traditions and pheonetics would resist change and keep a lingusitic inertia that creates an Ardulian Dialect of Cerik.

In practice a dialect speaker can understand a different dialect speaker if they are speaking the same root language, however the lilts and speach pattern would be easily recognizable to both trained and untrained ears. To avoid this, concious effort would have to be exerted to keep your speach neutral. 

As a conquoring language, cerik is resisted by many older traditions that resist occupation and cultural death. Though with time and the administrative requirement for solely its instruction and not the local languages, with time these forces of resistace tend to lose their grip on the local population. In general the closer a location is to the cerisian capitol or other large city, the more closely it follows the archtypal principles and cultural patterns of the cerisian empire.

Though as the language moves through the land it reccons with new discoveris, things, objects and concepts that cerik simply doesnt have. In these cases Cerik will often adopt the local term for that thing, names of plants, special objects, unique weapons and the like will sometimes become borrowed words in the expanding lexicon.

## Living Language

Cerik is a living Language. The majority of the population of the Cerisian empire and all that do business with them speak it. Though this is easier made clear in writing and recording. Thoes who are taught to write, learn from clasical examples and inevitably pick up clasical habbits in their cadence. This is less a problem in Abbies and monistaries where writeing is more common, but one reviewing texts with a anthropological eye would be forgiven for assuming a wide spread use of a more noble prose. 

In truth, the Cerisian empires success in its expansionist ideals have lead to a thinning grasp of its own language. each pocket taking and giveing to the language the words and weights of its people, such that 25 miles west of any one place may sound the the inexperienced travaler, rather forighn.
