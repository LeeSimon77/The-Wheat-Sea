# Sea of Wheat — Weapons

**Size** determines Carry Capacity per [Genaral.md](General.md): Small = 1, Medium = 2, Large = 4. Large weapons require both hands; cannot use a shield or buckler.

---

## Keywords

- `Civilian` — passable as an ordinary tool; can be carried openly without drawing suspicion
- `Hidden` — small enough to conceal on your person; it is not apparent that you are armed until the weapon is drawn
- `Loading` — can only fire once per Attack action, regardless of extra attacks
- `Reload` — requires a full action to reload after firing; cannot fire again until reloaded
- `Slow Reload` — requires 1 minute to reload; cannot be done in combat without assistance
- `Cavalry` — designed for mounted use; no penalty when used on horseback
- `Anti-Armour` — reduces target's effective Hardness by 4 on a blocked hit

---

## Mastery Properties

Mastery properties apply only when a character has proficiency and mastery training with the weapon. One mastery property may be applied per attack.

| Property | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Cleave** | On a hit, make one additional attack against a different creature within the weapon's reach. No bonus action required; once per turn. |
| **Graze** | On a miss, the target still takes damage equal to your STR or DEX modifier (minimum 1). |
| **Nick** | When the weapon grants an additional quick attack, you may make that attack as part of your Attack action rather than a bonus action. |
| **Push** | On a hit, push the target up to 10 ft away from you. |
| **Sap** | On a hit, the target has disadvantage on its next attack roll before the start of your next turn. |
| **Slow** | On a hit, the target's Speed is reduced by 10 ft until the start of your next turn. |
| **Topple** | On a hit, the target must succeed on a CON save (DC 8 + proficiency bonus + STR or DEX modifier) or be knocked prone. |
| **Vex** | On a hit, you have advantage on your next attack roll against the same target before the end of your next turn. |

### Descriptions

**Sling** — A strip of leather or cord used to hurl stones or lead shot.

**Shortbow** — A simple bow, quick to draw.

**Hunting Bow** — A bow made to hunt medium to large game. Common enough in any village; no special strength or training required. Lacks the draw weight to reliably pierce armour.

**Warbow** — A tall bow requiring significant strength to draw. Exceptional range and speed. difficult to inpossible to draw quickly.

**Composite Bow** — A recurve bow built from laminated horn, wood, and sinew. Shorter than a warbow with equivalent draw weight; can be drawn from horseback.

**Light Crossbow** — A mechanical bow that holds its draw, allowing a soldier to aim without sustained effort. One shot per Attack action; requires a full action to reload.

**Heavy Crossbow** — A heavier crossbow requiring a belt hook or goat's-foot lever to draw. More powerful; slower. One shot per Attack action; requires a full action to reload.

**Arbalest** — A crossbow with a steel prod drawn by a windlass. Can punch through plate armour at close range. One shot, then 1 minute to reload.
