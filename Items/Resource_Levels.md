# Sea of Wheat — Resource Levels

A player's **Resource Level** is a 0–10 slider representing their wealth and purchasing power. See Changelog entry 12 for the full rules on money checks and acquisition.

**Decay rate** is how long before a lifestyle becomes unsustainable without active income or a position that supports it.

---

| Level | Title | Decay to Previous |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 0 | Destitute | None |
| 1 | Poor | 1 month |
| 2 | Peasant | 2 months |
| 3 | Doing Well | 2 months |
| 4 | Well Off | 2 months |
| 5 | Wealthy | 6 months |
| 6 | Bourgeois / Low Nobility | 6 months |
| 7 | Noble | 12 months |
| 8 | High Noble | Immediate on loss of position |
| 9 | Noble with Claim | Immediate on loss of position |
| 10 | Sovereign | Immediate on loss of position |

---

## Level 0 — Destitute

Rags for clothes and little else.

- **Example items:** Pocket lint
- **Example NPCs:** Beggars, hermits
- **How to maintain:** Nothing
- **Decay:** None (floor)

---

## Level 1 — Poor

Rough, patched clothes. Thin food. Meagre accommodations.

- **Example items:** A day's food, cheap tools
- **Example NPCs:** Serfs, day laborers, street cleaners
- **How to maintain:** Cheap labor
- **Decay:** 1 month

---

## Level 2 — Peasant

Solid but simple clothes. Simple food. Your own tools. A small dwelling.

- **Example items:** A tavern room for a day, well-made tools, simple weapons
- **Example NPCs:** Farmers, skilled workers, servants, apprentices
- **How to maintain:** Skilled labor
- **Decay:** 2 months

---

## Level 3 — Doing Well

Good but simple clothes. Good boots. Food with some spices. A fair-sized dwelling. A small business of your own. Simple armour.

- **Example items:** A martial weapon, a complete tool set
- **Example NPCs:** Farmers (prosperous), innkeepers, simple shopkeeps, journeyman laborers with steady work, trained soldiers, maids, low-level mages
- **How to maintain:** Journeyman labor, business income
- **Decay:** 2 months

---

## Level 4 — Well Off

Comfortable clothes with some embellishment. Comfortable boots. Excellent food. Large dwelling. A large business. A standard servant or two.

- **Example items:** A basic set of armour, a small dwelling, a complex tool, a simple masterwork weapon
- **Example NPCs:** Large innkeepers, well-off shopkeeps, master laborers, ranking officers, employed mages, reputable mercenaries, head butlers
- **How to maintain:** Master labor, business income, duties
- **Decay:** 2 months

---

## Level 5 — Wealthy

Bespoke clothes with good embellishments. Excellent shoes. A chef in house. A large fully-staffed house. A position of minor power.

- **Example items:** A martial set of armour, a fair-sized building, a masterwork martial weapon, a set of complex or arcane tools, enchantment services
- **Example NPCs:** Knights, ranking mages, wealthy shopkeeps, workshop owners, large guildsmen
- **How to maintain:** Holding a position with a wealthy income
- **Decay:** 6 months

---

## Level 6 — Bourgeois / Low Nobility

Rich by definition. Best available versions of common items. Multiple homes or a large mansion. Well-paid and well-trained employees manage your home and estate. A small group of guards.

- **Example items:** Bespoke jewellery
- **Example NPCs:** Business owners, nobility, legendary mercenaries, respected mages, well-known government officials
- **How to maintain:** Holding a position with a wealthy income
- **Decay:** 6 months

---

## Level 7 — Noble

Wealth comes from territory or government position. Managing cities, towns, entire guilds — individual item costs are no longer a concern. Your last name is well known.

- **Example items:** Very rare items, plate armour
- **Example NPCs:** Nobility, archmages
- **How to maintain:** Be noble or an archmage
- **Decay:** 12 months

---

## Level 8 — High Noble

Inconceivably wealthy. You don't work so much as manage existing money and rank. In charge of large territories or hold chairs at important councils. Heads of storied houses and leaders of large armies. Wealth is no longer a pursuit — time is focused on power.

- **Example items:** Castles
- **Example NPCs:** High nobility
- **How to maintain:** High nobility (position-locked)
- **Decay:** Immediate on loss of position

---

## Level 9 — Noble with Claim

Little difference in wealth from a high noble, but greater political power. Somewhere in your family's history, you married into or are close to the royal line — or you hold power equivalent to it. You may rule a kingdom in shadow with a puppet emperor.

- **Example items:** Equivalent to High Noble
- **Example NPCs:** Dukes, kings of small kingdoms
- **How to maintain:** Be a duke (position-locked)
- **Decay:** Immediate on loss of position

---

## Level 10 — Sovereign

There is little to no opposition to you. Both wealth and power are yours to wield as you wish.

- **Example items:** Nothing — you have everything
- **Example NPCs:** Kings, tyrants, emperors, conquerors
- **How to maintain:** Be a king (position-locked)
- **Decay:** Immediate on loss of position
